Authorized by the Dorenshire Expeditionary Council

The old world is not gone. It sleeps. Beneath the silence of the Ruin, beneath ash and root and the long quiet years Sanctum endures, waiting for those bold enough to return, to reclaim what was lost, and to face whatever now walks in its ruins. The Sanctum Reclamation Initiative was founded upon a single conviction: that the world our ancestors built is worth the cost of getting it back. We are looking for those who share that conviction, and the courage to act on it.

Dorenshire cannot send an army. It can send you. Skilled individuals. Survivors. Dreamers with a weapon hand and enough sense to know when to run. The Initiative has chartered three expeditionary companies to establish footholds across Sanctum's western continent, map its terrain, catalogue its dangers, and push back the darkness that has taken root in our absence. You will be compensated. You will be equipped. And you will be part of something that history will remember.

⚠ Threat Assessment
Intelligence gathered by the Avandrian Academy confirms the presence of the Collector — a practitioner of otherworldly magic of unknown origin, who has established dominion over a significant portion of the western continent. The Collector is not of Sanctum. He moves between worlds, absorbing their secrets, capturing their creatures, and discarding what no longer interests him. With the abilities to open rifts on his side, he is believed to be working toward unmaking the Ancients' legacy using Sanctum as a staging ground, but his plans won't just unmake the Ancients, it will be the end of us all.

He is extraordinarily dangerous. He has powers beyond any one of us can fathom, but we can prevail with our efforts combined. His servants work under coercion or fear, but we are a unified force.

The continent is wild and largely unmapped. Its wildlife has been warped and provoked by the Collector's influence. Buildings from before the Ruin dot its interior, some intact, some occupied. You will face genuine peril. We will not pretend otherwise. What we will promise is this: the Initiative's three commanders are the finest minds and boldest leaders Dorenshire has ever produced. Under their direction, and with the collective strength of every expeditioner who answers this call, the Collector can be driven from our world.

Section I
Making Your Character

You will begin play at Level 3. At this level you have enough experience to survive the frontier but enough runway ahead to grow into something genuinely formidable. The continent will challenge you accordingly.

Your backstory should explain at minimum why you are here. The Sanctum Reclamation Initiative is a real organization with real stakes. Your character made a choice to join an expedition into a ruined and dangerous world. That choice should mean something. Were you born on Dorenshire and grew up hearing stories of the old world? Did you come through a rift and have nothing left to go back to? Are you chasing something? Knowledge, glory, a debt, a name? You don't need a ten-page biography. You need a reason that feels true.

On secrets: You are welcome, encouraged, even, to have things your character has not told anyone (if you've played in the Legacy of Winterhome campaign I ran, something like that). A history they've buried. An allegiance that isn't in the paperwork. A reason for being here that goes beyond what they've said out loud. The campaign will give those secrets room to breathe. Just make sure you know what they are, even if no one else does yet.

You are all arriving on the same continent, under the same Initiative charter, at the same time. You may know each other already, fellow Academy graduates, old traveling companions, rivals who ended up on the same ship; or you may be meeting for the first time. Either works. Talk to the other players before session one if you want to establish prior connections.

  • ✦Level 3, standard character creation rules. Use 27 point buy for stats. All official D&D content is allowed for character creation.
  • ✦A backstory that explains your presence. It doesn't need to be long. And remember to make your backstory fit with a 3rd level character.
  • ✦A secret, if you want one. Something only you know. Something that might matter later. Or maybe you really are just an open book.
  • ✦A relationship, if you want one. Coordinate with another player if you'd like a shared history coming in.
Section II
Choose Your Lord

Before your first session, you will declare which of the three commanders you are primarily aligned with. This is not a contract written in blood, alliances shift, circumstances change, and the campaign will give you reasons to reconsider. But you are starting somewhere, and where you start matters.

Think of it less as choosing a boss and more as choosing a value system or your preferred playstyles (though each faction will have a mix of all 3). Each commander represents something. The people around them reflect it. When you're deciding what your character would do in a hard moment, your lord's philosophy is the gravity you're pulling against or alongside.

You are free to take quests from any company, build friendships across faction lines, and maintain relationships with all three lords throughout the campaign.

Section III
How Sessions Work

This is a West Marches campaign. If you haven't played in one before, the core idea is this: there is no fixed party, no fixed schedule, and no expectation that everyone attends every session. The world exists and moves whether you are at the table or not. You show up when you can, you go on an expedition, and life on the frontier continues.

Each session represents one week of in-world time. A group of players will gather, take a contract or pursue a lead, venture into the continent, and return. That is one week. While they are gone, everyone else is living their life at the base camp, taking faction actions, building relationships, managing whatever the world is handing them.

You are not expected to attend every session. Life happens. The campaign is designed with it as the guiding philosophy. What matters is that when you are there, you are present, and that between sessions, you stay connected to the world through Discord roleplay and faction actions.

✦

Sessions will be scheduled by player interest. When a group wants to run a mission, they coordinate a time, declare their intent to the DM, and we play. Missions can be anything: a contract from one of the companies, a lead someone discovered in the previous session, a personal quest, a response to something happening in the world. You drive the agenda.

There are no railroads. There is also no guarantee of safety. The continent is genuinely dangerous, and not every expedition will go as planned. Come prepared. Communicate with your party. And remember that the choices your group makes on a mission will have consequences, for the world, for the NPCs involved, and sometimes for players who weren't there.

Section IV
Between Sessions

The campaign lives on Discord between sessions. This is where the world breathes. News travels. Relationships deepen. Plots develop. You are not required to participate at any particular intensity — some players will be active daily, others will check in occasionally — but the more you engage, the more the world will feel like yours.

Between sessions, time passes in real time. If a week passes in the real world between sessions, a week has passed in the world. Events occur. NPCs make decisions. The Collector's influence shifts. Faction politics evolve. Things that happened in the last session have had time to settle or escalate. Treat the Discord not as a waiting room but as the campaign itself, but a slower, quieter version of it.

What you can do between sessions: Roleplay with other players and NPCs in character. Pursue personal storylines through text RP with DM involvement. Gather information, build trust, or sow chaos. Spend time with your lord and their inner circle. Follow up on things that happened in the last session. The world is available to you, at least, your base camp is.

Significant between-session roleplay may have mechanical consequences, information gained, relationships built or damaged, small actions that ripple into future sessions. Pay attention. The campaign rewards engagement, and not always in the ways you expect.

Section V
Faction Actions

When you are not on an active expedition, you take a Faction Action — a structured choice about how your character spends their week at base (if you're in my Fire Emblem campaign, you'll recognize this). Faction Actions represent the non-adventuring work of building a foothold on a new continent: strengthening infrastructure, gathering intelligence, training, trading, diplomacy. They are how the base camp grows.

Build

Contribute labor and resources toward a specific base upgrade, fortifications, resource production, a proper infirmary, expanded stables. Build actions accumulate toward unlocking new capabilities for everyone.

Oversee

Oversee resource production, stockpile supplies. Oversee actions drive output from buildings in your camp, whether to generate resources that fuel future expeditions and faction projects or otherwise.

Train

Spend the week in intensive preparation. Train actions provide experience for your character, letting you keep up pace in levels even if you don't participate in sessions as often.

Investigate

Sometimes the story will require you to spend some time to follow leads, investigate clues, and rather than playing out every detail, you're just going to have to devote your time to get it done.

Full faction action rules — including specific mechanical outcomes and resource costs, will be in the relevant Discord channel. This mechanic ensures that you will always have something meaningful to do even when you can't make a session.

Section VI
The Chronicle

Every expedition will be documented in The SRI Field Chronicle, an in-world record kept by the Initiative's designated chronicler, posted to the Discord for all players to read after each session. The Chronicle is written from an in-world perspective: it is the official record, which means it reflects what the Initiative was told happened, not necessarily the full truth.

The Chronicle exists so that players who missed a session can stay informed, and so that players who were there can see their actions reflected in the world's memory. It also means that information can be managed, if your group comes back from an expedition and decides not to report certain things, those things won't be in the Chronicle. Other players won't know unless they find out some other way.

Secrets are possible. The Chronicle records what is reported. What is not reported remains unknown, until it isn't. If your group discovered something you'd rather keep between yourselves, you can choose what makes it into the official record. Bear in mind that other parties on the same continent may eventually find the same things, and the world does not wait for you to decide what to do with what you know.

Read the Chronicle, or don't. Even if you were on the expedition, there may be things you forgot about or simply didn't see. The picture of this continent is assembled from many partial views. Yours is only one of them.

This campaign is built on the choices you make in sessions and in the quiet moments between them
when you decide what kind of person your character is when no one important is watching.

Faction One
Ironward Logistics
"Merit earns the march."
⚙

Ironward is the largest and best-resourced of the three companies. It recruits on ability alone, no noble blood, no questions about the past, no loyalty owed to anyone who didn't earn it. Its camps are orderly, its logistics are precise, and its leadership is the most intellectually formidable on the continent. Ironward does not ask you to believe in anything. It asks you to perform.

Calder Voss
Director of Operations · Ironward Logistics
FounderMale Human, Age 39
Assessment
The most dangerous mind on the continent. Calder Voss built Ironward from nothing on a single conviction, that talent is the only currency that matters, and has never once wavered from it. His logistical networks keep camps alive in conditions that should be fatal. His intelligence operations know things before they happen. He is also a man who has decided, somewhere along the way, that the ends justify the means more often than most people are comfortable with. He will not announce this. He will simply proceed.
Known Traits
Unshakeable composure under pressure. Genuinely respects competence wherever he finds it, including in enemies. Uncomfortable with sentiment, not because he lacks it, but because he considers it a liability. Has advisors he trusts and gives them genuine room to operate. Slow to anger. When he is finally angry, it is architectural: deliberate, structural, designed to last.
Aldrin Dunmore
Field Commander · Ironward Logistics
CommanderFemale Human, Age 35
Assessment
One-eyed, unhurried, and possessed of the specific calm that belongs only to people who have already survived everything that should have killed them. Aldrin Dunmore has been with Calder long enough to push back on him, and loyal enough that she almost never does. Even if their job is logistics, Aldrin is the reason why Ironward will also be able to pull their weight in a battle against the Collector.
Known Traits
Blunt. Respectful of demonstrated courage regardless of rank. Her relationship with Calder is the oldest in Ironward; she is one of the few people who knows the difference between who he is and who he presents as, and has made her peace with both.

Enormous. Quiet. Terrifying in the specific way of things that do not feel the need to announce themselves. He will follow orders from Ironward's chain of command when those orders align with Calder's wellbeing. When they conflict, the chain of command does not win.
Known Traits
Does not waste words. Has a dry, very occasional sense of humour that surprises people who assumed he had none. Observes everything. Forgets nothing. Is one of perhaps three people on the continent Calder Voss visibly relaxes around. If Davan is concerned, something is wrong.

Precise in his language in a way that reflects how precisely he thinks. Idealistic but not naive, he understands exactly what Ironward and Calder is capable of, and has made a choice to work within it rather than walk away because he believes his presence keeps the institution closer to what it claims to be.
Gia Hollow

The one person in Ironward that Calder Voss listens to without reservation. Brilliant in a way that is somehow also irritating, she reaches correct conclusions through routes nobody else sees, then presents them with the casual energy of someone describing the weather. Faction Two
Hearthsworn Company
"We go together or not at all."
🜂

The smallest of the three companies and the one people seem to believe in most. Hearthsworn does not run on systems or bloodlines, it runs on something harder to quantify: the sense that these people would genuinely die for one another, and mean it. It was built by three childhood friends who came up from nothing and have never once stopped having each other's backs. It is the faction for people who need the cause to matter as much as the outcome.

Loric Ashvane
Commander and Co-founder · Hearthsworn Company
Co-founderMale Elf, Age 27
Assessment
Warm in the specific way that is indistinguishable from dangerous if you mistake warmth for weakness. Loric Ashvane has never asked anyone to follow him. They follow because he makes them believe that what they are doing together is worth the cost of doing it. He is principled to a degree that his allies find admirable and his enemies find exploitable, and he knows both are true and has decided it does not matter. His dream is not profit. He wants to actually help the people they find on this continent, and in the absence of people, he has expanded the definition to include the world itself.
Known Traits
Slow to judge, slow to condemn, impossible to corrupt through direct means. Can be moved by appeals to fairness that a more cynical leader would dismiss. Leans heavily on Gareth and Reni more than he admits. The gap between his instincts and the right answer is small.
Gareth Yu
Field Captain and Co-founder · Hearthsworn Company
Co-founderMale Human, Age 27
Assessment
Towering, deliberate, and in possession of a personal code of conduct that he has never once compromised in his life. Other warriors want Gareth's respect the way merchants want gold, it means something, because he does not give it carelessly. He is the moral anchor Hearthsworn moves around without fully acknowledging it does so. When he speaks in a meeting, the room listens. Not because of his rank. Because he is almost always right, and everyone in the room knows the last time he was wrong they wished they had listened.
Known Traits
A particular softness for people who have been betrayed by someone they trusted, he recognizes it and takes it seriously. Has a complicated relationship with patience: infinite toward people he is teaching, very limited toward people wasting his time.
Reni Calloway

Loud, impulsive, hilarious, and absolutely ferocious when it counts. Reni Calloway is the youngest of the founding trio and the one most likely to adopt a stranger as a lifelong companion within an hour of meeting them. She is also the one most likely to hold a grudge on behalf of those companions for decades after they've moved on. Her protectiveness over Loric and Gareth is the most uncomplicated thing about her. Despite her stature, she somehow feels like the biggest presence in a room.

Picks people up instantly and completely. Zero patience for condescension, social formality, or being underestimated because of how she presents. Has a temper that runs hot and cools quickly. Faction Three
Tidecrest Shipping
"Family holds the shore."
⚓

The oldest money of the three. Tidecrest was founded by the Solane family and has been a Solane company ever since, that is not nepotism, that is simply how they understand the world. Blood is the only thing you can truly trust, and everyone who earns a place under Tidecrest's banner becomes, in some operational sense, family. They are diplomatically sophisticated, strategically patient, and possessed of a network of merchants that none can match. They also protect their own with a ferocity that surprises people who mistook their civility for softness.
